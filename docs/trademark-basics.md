# Trademark Basics

## Definition

A **trademark** is a word, symbol, name, or design used to identify the source of goods or services.

Its primary purpose is to **distinguish one provider from another**.

<sup>
Trademark systems exist in most commercial legal frameworks around the world.
</sup>

---

## What Can Be Protected

A trademark may apply to several types of identity signals.

| Type       | Example                  |
| ---------- | ------------------------ |
| Brand name | company or product name  |
| Logo       | graphical symbol         |
| Phrase     | short identifying slogan |
| Symbol     | recognizable icon        |
| Packaging  | distinctive appearance   |

---

## Trademark Symbols

Common indicators include:

| Symbol | Meaning                              |
| ------ | ------------------------------------ |
| ™      | trademark claimed but not registered |
| ®      | officially registered trademark      |

<sup>
Using the registered symbol without registration may violate regulations in some jurisdictions.
</sup>

---

## Purpose of Trademark Protection

Trademark protection helps prevent:

* identity confusion
* misleading imitation
* reputation damage

These protections benefit both creators and consumers.

---

## Registration

In many jurisdictions trademarks can exist through **commercial use**, but formal registration provides stronger protection.

Registration generally provides:

* legal recognition
* clearer ownership
* easier enforcement
